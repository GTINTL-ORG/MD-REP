# Elysium — The Eternal City

**Lobby World · Neutral Ground · All races permitted**

The one place in all of Aethermoor where every race walks the same streets and every blade stays sheathed. Elysium is ancient, cosmopolitan, layered, and alive. It is the first thing every player sees — it must feel like stepping into a world worth staying in.

---

# Overall Atmosphere

Elysium is a city that has been building itself for three hundred years with no single race's aesthetic dominating. The result is layered and eclectic — Aethyn temple-arches sit beside Ironborn iron-framed shop fronts beside Sylvari-grown tree-canopies beside Umbrath curved-stone archways. It should feel lived-in, dense, and richly detailed. Not clean. Not orderly. Old, patched, expanded, and proud of it.

The city reads as medieval-with-touches-of-something-more — Ironborn engineering means there are mechanical lamp-posts, geared gates, and pulley-systems for cargo lifts. Sylvari influence means living trees grow through some buildings, vines wrap ironwork, and planters overflow from windows. The city smells of bread, iron, old stone, and forest. It sounds like a market.

---

# Key Structures to Build

## The Riftgate Plaza

Where players spawn. An open stone plaza with a large standing archway of Aetheric stone at its center — the Riftgate. The arch is taller than any building nearby, visibly ancient, carved with script in all five race languages. It glows faintly gold-silver at the edges.

The plaza around it has five pathways radiating outward, one toward each race district. A marble floor with the Accord of Five Seals carved into it.

> This is the first thing every player sees — it needs to feel monumental and welcoming simultaneously.

---

## The Wayfarer's Quarter

The district for new Travelers — players who haven't chosen a race.

Should feel like a transit hub meets a welcoming inn district.

Features:
- Small hostels
- Open-air taverns
- Notice boards covered in papers
- Merchants selling basic goods
- Warm lighting
- Slightly uneven cobblestone streets

Each race has a small embassy or office here — five distinct storefronts, each in its race's visual language, lined along the same street.

> The contrast is intentional — this is where all five worlds overlap.

---

## The Grand Market

Elysium's commercial heart.

A large open market square with permanent stone stalls and temporary canvas awnings.

Different sections sell different goods:

- Ironborn ore and tools
- Sylvari herbs and enchanted wood
- Umbrath shadow-crystals and information
- Ashen fire-salts and volcanic materials
- Aethyn divine-inscribed items

The market is loud and dense.

Overhead:
- Strings of lanterns

Underfoot:
- A maze of narrow cart paths

At the market's center:
- A large stone fountain with the five race crests carved into its basin.

---

## The Five-Race Embassy Row

A single wide avenue where each race maintains a formal embassy building.

Each building should be visually distinct and recognizable:

### Aethyn Embassy
- White stone
- Gold-script columns
- Open-sky windows

### Ironborn Embassy
- Iron-framed brick
- Mechanical doors

### Sylvari Embassy
- Grown wood walls
- Living roof

### Umbrath Embassy
- Curved black stone
- Few windows
- Always looks slightly closed even when open

### Ashen Embassy
- Dark stone
- Faint heat-shimmer details
- Fire-lit sconces

---

## The Lower City

The older, denser, less polished part of Elysium — the districts that grew organically before the Accord made the city official.

Features:
- Narrow alleys
- Buildings pressed against each other
- Laundry lines between upper floors
- Hidden rooms

A few Umbrath-influenced curved-stone buildings here look older than everything else.

The Lower City is where:
- Vex has their secret meeting room
- The underground market exists
- Players go when they want something the official city doesn't offer

> It should feel like it has secrets.

---

## The Accord Square

A formal square near the Conclave Hall.

Cleaner and more intentional than the rest of the city.

Features:
- Stone benches
- Sculpted hedges (Sylvari-maintained)
- Five race flags on permanent iron poles
- A large carved stone column bearing the text of the Accord of Five Seals

This is a public ceremonial space — events, declarations of war, and celebrations of peace all happen here.

> It should feel slightly formal and slightly melancholy — the weight of five ancient agreements in one place.

# Do / Don't

## ✅ Do

- Mix building styles — this city was built by five races over three centuries, not by one architect.
- Add small details:
  - Potted plants on ledges
  - Crates stacked in alleys
  - Notice boards with papers
- Use warm lighting:
  - Lanterns
  - Torches
  - Windows glowing amber at night
- Include Ironborn mechanical elements:
  - Geared lampposts
  - Iron fences
  - Pulley systems

---

## ❌ Don't

- Make it too clean or too symmetrical — this is a real city, not a showroom.
- Let one race's aesthetic dominate — the mixing is the point.
- Make it flat — vary building heights, add rooftop details, upper-floor walkways.
